## Summary of Signature Patterns Successfully Intercepted:

*   **Probability with Conditional Probabilities and Bayes' Theorem (HIT [CONCEPT]):** The Oracle correctly identified the prevalence of probability problems, particularly those involving conditional probabilities, Bayes' Theorem, and coin-toss types. It did well in framing probability problems with multiple events.
*   **Vectors: Dot and Cross Products (HIT [CONCEPT]):** The Oracle successfully predicted vector problems involving dot and cross products, unit vectors, orthogonality, and scalar triple products. Although the exact scenarios differed, the core concepts were well-represented.
*   **Definite Integral Properties (ALMOST SAME):** While the precise integrals were unique, the Oracle correctly identified the importance of definite integral properties, such as King's Rule, periodicity, and symmetry.
*   **Derivatives of Inverse Functions (HIT [CONCEPT]):** Several Oracle questions focused on finding the derivatives of inverse functions, a pattern that appeared in the actual exam in a slightly different form (implicit differentiation).
*   **Relations and Functions (HIT [CONCEPT]):**  The Oracle correctly predicted the importance of relations and functions, especially determining number of onto/one-to-one functions, and finding the domain of the function.
*   **Three Dimensional Geometry (HIT [CONCEPT]):** The Oracle correctly identified the importance of finding shortest distance between skew lines, direction cosines, sphere geometry and plane geometry.
*    **Determinants (ALMOST SAME):** The Oracle has questions on simplification and determinant properties. Actual paper had singularity concept.
*    **Integrals with Greatest Integer Functions (HIT [CONCEPT]):** The Oracle correctly identified GIF to be important in integrals.

The Oracle showed a strong ability to capture broad conceptual trends, but struggled with questions involving the application of core concepts in novel and unexpected ways.
