## COMPARATIVE ANALYSIS: WHY SUBJECTS PERFORMED DIFFERENTLY

### Physics Success Factors (76.2/100)

**Why Physics Ranked #1:**

1. **Finite Formula Space:** Physics relies on well-defined laws (F=ma, V=IR, E=hf) with limited variation, making conceptual prediction easier
2. **Standard Problem Types:** KCET physics heavily uses classic problems (charged particles in B field, YDSE maxima, Gauss's law flux)
3. **Predictable Exam Patterns:** Same physics principles reappear with different numerical values - perfect for AI pattern recognition
4. **Strong Curriculum Alignment:** REI-v17 appears well-trained on NCERT physics fundamentals
5. **Modern Physics Advantage:** Semiconductor, photoelectric, Bohr model - highly standardized topics with limited variation

**What Made It Easiest to Predict:**
- Most questions test **application of known formulas** rather than creative problem-solving
- **Conceptual frameworks are universal** - once you know the physics, numerical changes don't matter
- **Limited trap-based questions** - fewer deceptive options compared to Math

---

### Biology Success Factors (68.8/100)

**Why Biology Ranked #2:**

1. **Factual Nature:** Biology heavily tests recall (structures, functions, processes) which AI can catalog systematically
2. **Standard NCERT Examples:** Many questions use textbook organisms, diagrams, and examples (E.coli, pBR322, human insulin)
3. **Molecular Biology Standardization:** DNA fingerprinting, rDNA technology have fixed protocols easy to predict
4. **Limited Numerical Variation:** Unlike Physics/Chemistry, Biology has fewer calculation-heavy questions
5. **Process-Based Questions:** Sequencing steps (decomposition, insulin production) are highly predictable

**What Held It Back:**
- **Broad Factual Domain:** Anatomy details (GFR values, specific disease organisms) are harder to predict without exhaustive databases
- **Organism Diversity:** Exam tested specific organisms not in predicted sets (Funaria, specific bacterial pathogens)
- **Diagram Interpretation:** Age pyramids, pedigree charts require visual analysis not well-suited to current AI

---

### Chemistry Success Factors (68.8/100 - Tied with Biology)

**Why Chemistry Ranked #2 (Tied):**

1. **Organic Chemistry Predictability:** Mechanisms (SN1, SN2, aldol) and functional group tests are highly standardized
2. **Named Reactions:** Iodoform, Cannizzaro, Grignard - finite set of reactions KCET tests repeatedly
3. **Kinetics Formulas:** First-order, activation energy, Arrhenius - mathematical but standardized
4. **Electrochemistry Standardization:** Faraday's laws, Kohlrausch, Nernst - well-defined equations
5. **Comprehensive Coverage:** REI-v17 achieved 100% chapter coverage with no blind spots

**What Held It Back:**
- **Zero Exact Numerical Hits:** Every calculation used different numbers - suggests exam writers deliberately vary parameters
- **Quantum Mechanics Weak:** Structure of atom (ψ², de Broglie) conceptually challenging for AI
- **Biomolecules Diversity:** Vitamins, amino acids have many specific facts harder to catalog exhaustively

---

### Mathematics Challenge Factors (52.8/100)

**Why Mathematics Ranked #4:**

1. **Infinite Parameter Space:** Unlike physics formulas with standard values, math problems can use ANY numbers/coordinates/functions
2. **Creative Problem Design:** Math questions often use novel scenarios (viral video spread, drone motion) not in textbook templates
3. **Trap-Heavy Questions:** More deceptive options requiring deep conceptual understanding, not just formula application
4. **Definitional Questions:** Many "basic" questions (row matrix, determinant definition) don't fit formula-prediction models
5. **Diversity of Sub-Topics:** 15+ distinct areas (set theory, relations, permutations, G.P., trigonometry) each needing separate coverage

**What Made It Hardest to Predict:**
- **Novel Problem Formulations:** Even standard topics presented in unfamiliar ways
- **Combinatorics Unpredictability:** Subset counting, arrangements have infinite variations
- **Coordinate Geometry Specifics:** Area with specific vertices, line division ratios - infinite possibilities
- **Definitional Gaps:** AI skipped "too basic" questions that actually appeared in exam
